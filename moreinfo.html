A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015, Shillong) was an Indian scientist and politician who played a key role in India's missile and nuclear weapons programs, and served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
